Apply for a Cal Grant

Deadline to apply: Monday, March 2, 2020

Apply in two steps

A Cal Grant is money for college you don’t have to pay back. There are two steps to apply for a Cal Grant.

  • 1 Apply for financial aidShowHide

    Submit the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) or the California Dream Act Application (CADAA).
    Apply for financial aid →
  • 2 Submit your Cal Grant GPA form ShowHide

    You’ll fill out a one-page form and get it signed by an official from your high school or college. There’s no need to submit a transcript.
    Submit your GPA form →

Who can get a Cal Grant

To qualify for a Cal Grant, you must:

  • Be a California resident for one year or more
  • Attend an eligible California college
  • Not already have a bachelor’s or professional degree
  • Have U.S. citizenship, or be eligible for federal student aid, or be eligible for AB540
  • Need help to pay for your college’s tuition
  • Have family income and assets (such as savings) at or below the minimum
  • Be enrolled or plan to enroll in a program that leads to an undergraduate degree or certificate
  • Be enrolled or plan to enroll at least half time
  • Register with the U.S. Selective Service, if you’re required to register
  • Not owe a refund on any state or federal grant or be in default on a student loan
  • Not be in jail
  • Meet the Satisfactory Academic Progress standards set by your school
